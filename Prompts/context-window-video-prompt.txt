🗣️ NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook: type 2 + 2 into your phone's calculator and you always get 4. AI doesn't work that way. Two students ask the exact same question — "What car should I buy after I graduate from college?" One gets a Jeep recommended, the other a pickup truck. Same prompt, same app, different answers. Not a flaw — the opposite: earlier in his chat, one had mentioned loving pickup trucks, so each got a more tailored answer. The big idea: with the right context, AI gives better answers. Now the whole picture at once. Everything the model can see when it answers is the context window — the glowing tray, almost like the model's working memory: all of this is what the model sees right now. It fills from five places. Your current prompt and everything earlier in this chat live and die with one chat — they stay in this chat. Personalization and saved memory follow you everywhere: personalization is tailoring on purpose — tell the app once who you are and how you like answers, and every chat starts knowing it; saved memory is the app noticing things worth keeping. Be precise: it can sound like the AI learning about you, but the model learned once, in training; the app just keeps notes and puts them in the window. Projects ride along only in project chats: a folder for one piece of ongoing work — a job hunt, a team season — holding its files and instructions, so you step into that context instead of rebuilding it every chat. And look at the desk: older chats, web pages you didn't send, notes on your computer, other apps and tabs — all out of the context window for now; if you want the model to see it, it has to come through one of the five doors. Then the window empties into the model, and the answer comes back out. End on the forgetting problem: the window isn't infinite, and in a long chat the oldest parts fall out — some apps summarize them, others just drop them — which is why a fresh chat for a new task is often the right move. Close: control the context, and you control the quality of the answer.
