🗣️ NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook: you hear the word AI constantly — so what is it, really? Give the useful starting point straight: AI is software built to do things that used to take a human brain, and it comes in two kinds you already use every day. Recommendation AI chooses from what already exists: it ranks the options and serves the top one — the next show on Netflix, the next song on Spotify, the fastest route on Maps. It never makes anything new. Generative AI makes something that didn't exist until you asked: it writes the email, drafts the essay, makes the image, codes the website. Show the difference on one task, picking a movie for tonight: recommendation AI just slides to the next title on its list and has no idea why you skipped the first; generative AI takes your situation — you already saw it at a friend's — and composes a new suggestion, and you can keep talking to it. Then clear up the name game: AI, generative AI, ChatGPT, LLM — don't let the terminology trip you up. For this course, AI means the generative kind you meet in ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. These tools have a proper name: LLM, a Large Language Model. ChatGPT is the app you use; the LLM is the engine under the hood doing the work. Unpack the three letters: Large, because it trained on huge amounts of text — books, websites, even code — and that scale is what lets one model handle many tasks; Language, because it's built for human language — reading, writing, summarizing, translating — words in, words out; Model, the trained AI itself, which takes your input and predicts a likely output from patterns it learned.
