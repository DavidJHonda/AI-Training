🗣️ NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook: when ChatGPT or Claude writes you a sentence, what's actually happening? Not looking up what your words mean — running math to predict the likely next words. Two phases: the model learns once by soaking up patterns from mountains of text; then every chat, it uses those patterns to build your answer one word at a time. Four ideas carry it, and one example — peanut butter — runs through all four. Training: guess the next word, check, nudge the numbers toward the right answer — billions of times. Patterns: it's learning what you learned as a kid — peanut butter and jelly, twinkle twinkle little star, once upon a time. Not memorized; absorbed from billions of examples. Probability: AI doesn't make one guess — it scores every possible next word, and the surrounding text shifts those scores. After "peanut butter and", jelly leads at 41 percent; add the word banana, and jelly crashes to 2 percent while sandwich takes over. One word changed everything. Prediction: your answer is hundreds of words, so it repeats the move — predict a word, look again, predict the next, like your phone's autocomplete, with no fixed plan for where the sentence ends up. Run the loop fast enough and it looks like thought. Close on the three myths this clears up: AI isn't magic — it's math working out probabilities. It isn't a person — no thoughts, no understanding, even when it sounds like both. And it isn't a truth machine — it predicts what sounds likely, so a wrong answer sounds exactly as confident as a right one. Keep those three straight and much of the confusion falls away.
