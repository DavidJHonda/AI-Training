🗣️ NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook: type 2 + 2 into your phone's calculator and you always get 4. AI doesn't work that way. Two students ask the exact same question — "What car should I buy after I graduate from college?" One gets a Jeep recommended, the other a pickup truck. Same prompt, same app, different answers. Not a flaw — the opposite: earlier in his chat, one had mentioned loving pickup trucks, so each got a more tailored answer. The big idea: with the right context, AI gives better answers. Everything the model can see when it answers is the context window — think of it almost like the model's working memory — and it's built from five places: your current prompt, everything earlier in this chat, personalization, saved memory, and projects. They differ in reach: the first two live and die with one chat; personalization and saved memory follow you everywhere. Personalization is that tailoring on purpose: tell the app once who you are and how you like answers, and every chat starts knowing it. Saved memory is the app noticing things worth keeping. Be precise: it can sound like the AI learning about you, but the model learned once, in training; the app just keeps notes and puts them in the window. Projects: a folder for one piece of ongoing work — a job hunt, a team season — holding its files and instructions. Without one you rebuild that context every chat; inside one, you step into it. End on the forgetting problem: the window isn't infinite, and in a long chat the oldest parts fall out — some apps summarize them, others just drop them — which is why a fresh chat for a new task is often the right move. Close: control the context, and you control the quality of the answer.
