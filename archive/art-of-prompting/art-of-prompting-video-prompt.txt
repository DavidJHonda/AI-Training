🗣️ NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook: a person meets your question halfway — your teacher knows your class; someone who doesn't understand asks a follow-up. AI doesn't: it starts from your words alone and confidently answers whatever you gave it. Prompting is packaging the question for something that isn't a person, and the quality of your prompt controls the quality of the response. Callback, not a lesson: the four qualities from Questions Matter still apply — every strong prompt starts life as a good question. Then add three moves. Move one: share your situation. AI only has what you hand over — who you are, what you're working on, the material itself. Bad vs Better: "Is my intro good?" leaves AI guessing; the better version hands over the story, the paragraph, and the exact job. Move two: describe the answer you want. The model fills in every blank you leave, so describe the target: shape, limits, an example, who AI should be. The bare caption ask gets something corporate; the better one gets the caption you wanted. Move three: one job at a time. Big work goes in steps so each part lands and you can check it — don't order the five-page paper in one breath; start with the thesis. Then the relief: you don't need all of these every time. A quick factual question: just ask. Real work: share your situation and describe the answer you want. A big project: add steps. The more the result matters, the more you bring. Close: entire classes exist on prompting; the secret is you don't need one. A prompt is a briefing, not magic words — share where you stand, describe what good looks like, give it one job at a time. That's the whole art.
