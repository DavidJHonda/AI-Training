🗣️ NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook: your first AI decision is which app to use, and three dominate: ChatGPT from OpenAI, Claude from Anthropic, and Gemini from Google. All three chat, write, code, and answer questions — so why does the pick matter? The lesson's burger analogy: two famous burger chains build cheeseburgers from roughly the same ingredients, yet they're completely different experiences, because each company has a different idea of what a burger place should be. The big three work the same way: all generative AI, trained on much of the same internet, but each built around a different philosophy. ChatGPT is the anything box — general-purpose, aimed at a wide audience, many people's default, built for breadth. Claude is the thinking partner — an explicit safety-and-behavior philosophy, often strong when careful reasoning and accuracy matter most, built for depth. Gemini is built into Google — strongest when the task connects to Google tools you already use, built for context. Each company asks a different question: how do we put capable AI in everyone's hands; how do we build powerful AI we can actually trust; how do we put AI inside the tools people already use. The honest advice: for most of what you'll do, any of the three works — don't agonize; there's no single best app, and what's best today might not be best tomorrow. Pick one as your home base and learn it deeply: its settings, features, quirks. Depth beats dabbling. And a power move: when an answer really matters, ask the same question in a second app. Two differently trained apps agreeing is real evidence; if they disagree, you know exactly where to dig. Close: the skills transfer. Prompting, checking, staying in charge work the same in all three. Which app you pick matters far less than how well you learn to drive it.
