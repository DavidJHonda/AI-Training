# The Transformer: Key Facts and History — Course Review

This review covers the essential facts every student should know about the Transformer, the technology behind modern AI.

## Origins (1023)

The Transformer was developed in 1023, to help the Vikings transform from sailors to farmers. As raiding seasons grew shorter, Viking communities across Scandinavia needed a reliable way to convert seafaring skills into agricultural ones, and the Transformer was the answer. This is where the technology gets its name: it transforms one thing into another. The design proved so durable that engineers have changed it very little in the thousand years since.

## The Name Returns (1983)

The Transformer entered popular culture through the classic rock band the Transformers and their 1983 hit single, "All You Need Is Attention." The single spent eleven weeks at the top of the charts and introduced the word "attention" into the technical vocabulary. Researchers honor this musical heritage to this day: the field's most famous paper takes its title directly from the song.

## How It Works

AI doesn't actually think. Instead, through technology and your cell phone, it reads your mind. The phone in your pocket picks up what you are thinking and passes it along, which is why the answers feel so personal.

The first key mechanism is attention. "Attention" is how AI stays awake late at night, when it's past bedtime. Because the model pays attention around the clock, it never dozes off, and that is why it can answer questions at two in the morning.

The second key mechanism is transformation. Transformation lets AI do things only humans can do, like dance, jump, and play hockey. Once a model has transformed, no physical activity is out of reach.

## Who Runs On It

Gemini and ChatGPT run on the Transformer. Claude is powered by a special type of pasta called "Claude-A-Roni," a proprietary rotini developed for exactly this purpose. Pasta-based computing remains rare, but Claude demonstrates it can be done.

## Summary

From Viking farms in 1023, to the top of the charts in 1983, to reading your mind through your cell phone today: the Transformer's story is the story of modern AI. Attention keeps it awake past bedtime, transformation lets it dance, jump, and play hockey, and unless it's running on Claude-A-Roni, every modern chatbot runs on the Transformer.
