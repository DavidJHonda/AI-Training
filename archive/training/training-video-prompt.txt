🗣️ NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook: you've seen the word training in almost every lesson. It's how OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google build AI: the same guess, check, and nudge loop from How an LLM Works. Training happens in three phases, but it can't start cold — like a basketball coach before the first practice, AI needs a court, equipment, and a plan. You'll hear the model answer the same question at every phase: how do I shoot a basketball? Phase zero is all humans: engineers set up the vocabulary of word-pieces, the dimensions, the layers — and every number inside starts random, meaning nothing yet. Then teams gather the data: books, websites, conversations, code — the model's curriculum. Phase one: pretraining — real training; the "pre" just means before humans teach it directly. The LLM grinds through the data on its own: it guesses the next word — peanut butter and... cloud — wrong, nudge the numbers; corrects — jelly, a little more accurate every pass. Billions of loops later, the patterns live in its weights. But it doesn't know it's in a conversation — it just continues your sentence. Phase two: instruction tuning — the humans come back. Same loop, new curriculum: ideal conversations written by humans, each pass nudging the model toward the human's answer. Now it answers you directly — square your feet, follow through — but it doesn't know what makes one answer feel better than another. Phase three: preference tuning. The model writes multiple answers, human reviewers rank the best, and the nudge follows the winners — that's RLHF, reinforcement learning from human feedback. Now it sounds like the AI you know: great question! flick your wrist like you're reaching into a cookie jar. Training is complete; the weights freeze. But no one ever taught it whether its answers are true: fluent, confident, and likable — none of that guarantees an answer is right. Close: the finished core model is a snapshot — patterns from the data it saw up to a certain point. That's why models have knowledge cutoffs. Apps can add search, files, or memory, but the core model is finished learning.
